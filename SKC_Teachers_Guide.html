🔗 Joining the Session

  1. Go to skoolofcode.us and log in as a teacher.
  2. Click on the Start Session button next to the class you want to join — or click the class name first and then click Start Session.
Note: Kindly enable all pop-ups that appear when starting a session.

  • Teacher Dashboard
  • Student Dashboard
  • Parent Dashboard

Certificates are available only once the batch is marked as completed. Parents can also download previous certificates by changing tabs on the Parent Dashboard.

🎒 Once a New Batch is Assigned

Once a batch is assigned, you will be added to a WhatsApp group that includes parents and possibly students. All communication with parents must happen in the group only — no personal chats allowed.

Steps to Follow

  1. Introduction by Sunita: Ms. Sunita will send class details and login instructions for students.
  2. Teacher's Introduction Message: Within 12 hours of group creation, introduce yourself using the format below. Get it verified by the Area Head before sending.
Hello Young Coder,
I am Ms./Mr. [Your Name], your instructor for this course. You must be excited to embark upon this journey of learning [Module Name] 😊. Looking forward to meeting you at [Time] on [Date of First Class]. *Include your qualification and experience in short in the above message.

  • Avoid using direct names. In a 1:1 class, use "coder" (not "coders") unless you are confident about the spelling.
  • Use Grammarly to check messages before sending.
  • Communicate only between 9 AM – 9 PM India time.
  • Reply to parent queries within 8 hours.
  • Update class notes and status in the Topics tab within 2 hours of class completion.

🎥 Classroom Setup & Session Management

All sessions must be interactive and lively — not monotonous. Camera must be on during class.

Pre-Session (T-15)

  • Launch the session 15 minutes prior.
  • Send the T-15 WhatsApp message using the provided format.

Session Timing

  • Sessions are 1 hour long.
  • Avoid extending. If extension is necessary, obtain parents' consent first.

Post-Session Updates (within 2 hours)

  • Update class notes on the dashboard.
  • Update class status to "In Progress" or "Completed".

Class Conduct

  • Keep camera on from the moment students join.
  • Conduct live coding via the Zoom client/desktop app with the SKC virtual background.

Student Attendance

  • If student doesn't join within 5 minutes, send: "Waiting for <<student name>> to join the session" in the WhatsApp group.
  • If still absent, notify the SKC-ClassOps group. Wait up to 30 minutes from start time.

🧑‍💻 Classroom Dynamics

Session Structure

  • 1
    Warm-Up (1–2 min) Brief interaction to engage students.
  • 2
    Recap (5 min) Review key points from the previous lesson.
  • 3
    Topic/Activity Introduction (5 min) Introduce the new topic using Kenspark slides.
  • 4
    Live Coding with Student (30–35 min) Show project output first (not code). Guide students through screen share without spoon-feeding.
  • 5
    Testing & Debugging (5–10 min) Help students test and debug their code.
  • 6
    End Recap + Hometask Summarize key points and assign the home task.

Key Notes

  • Engagement: Keep the class interesting and engaging at all times.
  • Multiple Students: Enable multiple screen shares. Use annotations to explain and correct. Reference: YouTube Video on multiple screen share.
  • Difficult Concepts: Share your screen to explain first, then let students replicate.
  • Student-Centric: Avoid doing the project yourself every time. Encourage students to code — avoid copy-paste learning.

📝 Home Task

Importance

Homework helps students study independently and solidify their understanding. Use Kenspark to assign tasks, plus additional coding tasks based on concepts taught.

Motivation & Feedback

  • Encourage students using reminders — avoid nagging.
  • Provide constructive feedback. Avoid blaming or warning students.

Friendly Reminder to Parents (if student never does hometask)

Dear Parents,
A friendly reminder: please encourage your child to complete the practice task assigned. This will help reinforce his/her learning and ensure his/her stay on track.
Thank you for your support.
Best regards,
[Teacher Name]

Sharing Feedback

Share individual feedback in a way that does not embarrass the student. Maintain a respectful and supportive environment at all times.

✅ Assessment

  • Timing: During the last 10–15 minutes of a class, after every few classes.
  • Task: Provide a coding task or assign an assessment on Kenspark.
  • Teacher Presence: The teacher must be actively present throughout the assessment period.

🎤 Presentation Day

Preparation

  • Conduct once all module topics are completed (or about to be completed).
  • Inform students one week prior and share presentation guidelines.
  • Students can pick any activity from the current module.

Presentation Guidelines for Students

  • Duration: 2–3 minutes per student.
  • Share first name only — no last name, school name, country, or city.
  • State the topic to present.
  • Show the final output in full screen.
  • Explain any part of the code.
  • Thank the viewers.

💬 Communication with Parents

General Approach

  • Be straightforward, polite, and constructive.
  • Avoid arguments and confrontational exchanges.
  • Listen actively and keep discussions short and crisp.

Handling Suggestions

  • Acknowledge suggestions with appreciation.
  • Assure parents their feedback will be communicated to the relevant person. Loop in the Area Head in such cases.

Addressing Inquiries

  • Provide clear, helpful responses.
  • If unsure, admit it and commit to following up with accurate information. Loop in the Area Head.

Suggested Replies When Uncertain

  • "Well, that's good feedback, I will surely convey it to the concerned person."
  • "I am not too sure about it, will confirm and get back to you as soon as possible."
  • "I understand, please give us time to rectify the issue."

🌻 Progress Discussion (Last Class of Module)

Before PD — 15 Minutes Prior

Dear Parents, Please join the progress discussion as per the scheduled time. Thank you.
Toggle on the "PTM" switch before starting the session. The recording won't be visible on the dashboard but will still be recorded.

PTM Conversation Guide (for Reference)

  1. Welcome the parent and state the purpose of the meeting.
  2. Play the student's video presentation.
  3. Ask for consent to upload the video on the showcase page. If yes, click the "Consent" button on the dashboard and guide them to reply to the email.
  4. Share positive feedback about the student, including areas of improvement in an encouraging way.
  5. Ask the parent for their feedback on the teaching approach.
  6. Show the dashboard — explain concepts covered and preview the next module.
  7. Ask for a Google/Trustpilot review: "If you've been pleased with our service, we would sincerely appreciate you taking a moment to rate us on Google/Trustpilot." Share the link in the group and guide them through the steps.
  8. End PTM session and relaunch a regular session within 5 minutes for the student.

Post-PTM Actions

  • Rejoin without the "PTM" toggle — continue regular class for remaining time.
  • Add class notes and mark the course as complete.

📅 Class Cancellations / Rescheduling — Teacher's End

Finding a Substitute

  1. Inform your Area Head of your unavailability.
  2. Try to find a substitute teacher who will take the class on your behalf.
  3. Once confirmed, email the operations team (Sunita) and CC your Area Head — include class time, substitute's name, and relevant details. Send at least 8–10 hours prior to the class.

If No Substitute is Available

Post a rescheduling message in the WhatsApp group at least 8–10 hours prior:

Dear Parent,
Due to unavoidable personal/technical reasons, we need to reschedule the class. The time slots available are as follows:
Slot 1: Slot 2:
Kindly let me know which of the above slots suits you.
We regret the inconvenience.
Regards,
[Teacher's Name]
Use a general reason such as "Personal reason" or "Technical reason." Avoid providing specific details.

🔁 Class Cancellations / Rescheduling — Parent's End

  1. Cancellation After Session Start (or within 8 hours):
    Not required to offer a reschedule unless it's a medical emergency. Reply:
    Hi [Parent's Name],
    Apologies, sir/ma'am. Since this is a last-minute cancellation, rescheduling will not be feasible. We will meet [Student's Name] at the same time next week as per the regular schedule.
    However, if you anticipate any scheduling conflicts in the future, please let us know at least a day in advance, and we'll be happy to accommodate a reschedule. Thank you for your understanding!
    Looking forward to the next session. 😊
    Best regards,
    [Teacher's Name] SkoolOfCode
    Even if you launched the session, add class notes. If the student didn't join, add: "The student/students was/were unable to participate in the scheduled class today. We hope to reconnect in the next session."
  2. Cancellation 8+ Hours in Advance:
    Provide your available slots for rescheduling.
  3. >In-Class Cancellation:
    After the session, send in the WhatsApp group: "As discussed in class, we will be canceling/rescheduling this session to [new date and time]."
  4. >Permanent Schedule Change:
    Drop a message in the group: "As discussed in class, we will be having the sessions on [new date and time] from the upcoming week."
